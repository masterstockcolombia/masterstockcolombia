SAMPLE

Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

BETWEEN
MasterStock USA Inc. (“MS”)
AND
[Supplier Legal Name] (“Supplier”)

1. Purpose. The parties intend to explore a potential transaction involving the sale of excess or liquidation inventory (the “Transaction”) and may exchange confidential information (“Confidential Information”) in connection with evaluating it.

2. Confidential Information. Includes any non-public business, financial, operational, technical, product, or pricing information disclosed by one party to the other, whether oral, written, or electronic, marked or reasonably identified as confidential.

3. Obligations. Each party agrees to (a) use Confidential Information solely to evaluate the Transaction; (b) protect it using at least the same standard of care it uses for its own confidential information; (c) limit disclosure to employees, advisors, and affiliates with a need-to-know, who are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

4. Exclusions. Confidential Information does not include information that (i) is or becomes publicly available through no breach; (ii) was rightfully known before receipt; (iii) is independently developed; or (iv) is required to be disclosed by law, subject to prompt notice to the disclosing party.

5. Term. Obligations under this Agreement survive for twenty-four (24) months from the Effective Date.

6. Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, USA.

— End of sample. Full executable version available on request.

SAMPLE

Bill of Sale · Excess Inventory Transaction

SELLER: [Supplier Legal Name]
BUYER: MasterStock USA Inc., Delaware
DATE: [Transaction Date]

1. Sale. Seller hereby sells, transfers, and conveys to Buyer all right, title, and interest in the inventory described in Schedule A (Manifest), attached and incorporated herein (the “Inventory”).

2. Consideration. Buyer has paid Seller $[Total Purchase Price] in US dollars, wired from Buyer's US Mercury Treasury account on the Date above. Payment structure per the executed Offer (Full-Lot / Selective Buy / Floor + Upside).

3. Condition & Title. Seller warrants that (a) Seller holds clear title free of liens, encumbrances, and claims; (b) Inventory is in the condition represented in the Manifest; (c) Seller has full authority to sell.

4. Channel Restrictions. Buyer agrees to the channel restrictions checked in Schedule B: ☐ No Amazon ☐ No eBay ☐ No brand-owned stores ☐ Export only ☐ Region-specific. Violations are subject to the penalties stated in Schedule B.

5. Risk of Loss. Title and risk transfer to Buyer at the moment of pickup at origin dock.

6. Inspection Reconciliation. Seller acknowledges that Buyer performs a 7-day video-recorded inspection against the Manifest. Line-item variances resolved per the reconciliation schedule in Schedule C.

7. Tax Documentation. Buyer will provide a W-9 upon request and a complete documentation package (Bill of Sale, manifest reconciliation, wire confirmation, condition report). Seller's tax treatment of this transaction is determined solely by Seller's CPA, enrolled agent, or tax attorney based on Seller's facts and entity structure. Buyer makes no representation as to deductibility or tax outcome.

8. Governing Law. Delaware. Disputes over $5,000 USD resolved by binding arbitration (AAA or JAMS), loser pays.

— End of sample. Schedules A, B, C customized per transaction.

Tax documentation

Every deal closes with the paperwork your CPA expects.

When excess inventory sells below cost, the loss flows through your Cost of Goods Sold — that's ordinary inventory accounting, not a special provision. What matters is having the documentation to support it: a clean Bill of Sale, a reconciled manifest, wire confirmation, and the pickup dated to the period you need it in. We ship all four with every deal, at no additional cost.

This is not tax advice. MasterStock USA provides transactional documentation only. We do not determine tax treatment, prepare tax returns, or give tax opinions. Your entity structure, inventory method, and applicable deductions are determined solely by your CPA, enrolled agent, or tax attorney based on your specific facts.

Standard with every transaction

Every deal ships with this documentation package. No upsell, no tier. Included.

Bill of Sale

Line-item detail: SKU, quantity, condition grade, sale proceeds, pickup date, signed by both parties. The document of record for the transaction.

Manifest reconciliation

Our count vs. your manifest, with any shortages, damages, or adjustments explicitly called out. Clean audit trail, no surprises.

Wire confirmation

Originated from our US Mercury Treasury account. Proof of payment, not just a journal entry. Named counterparty for arm's-length documentation.

Condition grading report

A / B / Salvage grading per SKU or lot, with notes on packaging, seals, and observable defects. Supports fair market documentation.

Resale Certificate

Where applicable by state, so you do not charge sales tax on the transaction. We provide the correct form for your jurisdiction.

Timing that matches your fiscal year

Tell us your fiscal year-end. We engineer the pickup window to land the transaction in the tax period you need it in. Standard for Q4 close, available for any fiscal pattern.

Available on request

For lots that are partially destroyed or routed to charitable partners, we issue additional certificates your CPA may need for alternative tax positions.

Certificate of Destruction

For portions of the lot we destroy (expired, damaged, or brand-restricted). Dated, signed, with line-item detail.

Charitable diversion attestation

For portions routed to qualified 501(c)(3) partners, including the recipient organization's EIN and acceptance letter.

Why this matters

CPAs bill real hours to reconstruct it. We deliver the package complete so your tax team can focus on the return, not on chasing paperwork.
